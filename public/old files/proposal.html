PC Builder

Problem Description

The topic of our application is personal computer (PC) building. Given the wide variety of PC components available, each type of component comes in numerous variations, featuring distinct specifications and is uniquely manufactured by various brand. This wide array of components and the incompatibility of many parts makes PC building a confusing process for both experienced and inexperienced PC builders. Yes, you can watch hundreds of tutorials and eventually learn about each component, brand, their compatibility, specifications, and how to put together a PC, but that takes hours and memorization.

To avoid the confusion, many people rely on websites like PC Part Picker to find and select the component they want. This website allows users to select parts for their PC build, view PC's that other people have built themselves, and has guides to building quality PC's. However, several issues that our group discovered with this website is that it can be difficult to build and compare similar configurations around a certain dollar amount, and it does not have any way to know or avoid bottlenecks.

Due to the potential hassle of physically building a PC, many people go with the pre-built PC route. This is where a manufacturer builds a complete PC that is ready for consumers to purchase. However, this route has its drawbacks. Pre-built PC's often come with a higher pricetag (compared to a self-built PC with similar components), poorer quality components, possibly lower performance, and possibly lack of customization and upgradability.

App Description

Our app aims to address these problems. Our website will target a demographic that is more novice towards knowing computer components or is lacking adequate knowledge towards what parts are compatible with other parts while avoiding bottlenecks. We want to allow users to both achieve the cheaper and higher-quality components of a custom-built, with the reliance and peace of mind of a pre-built. However, because of our focus on demographic, this app may not have many more features than PC Part Picker has for experts in PC-building, but content and features such as already-created builds from experts using online parts, and ease of customizing multiple builds to work around a budget, should allow for newcomers to confortably build their own PC.

The features that we are looking to implement are:

  • Custom Build Comparison
  • Build Guides
  • Part Selector and Custom PC Builder
  • (Possible) Upload Custom Builds Forum

The custom build comparison feature allows for users who sign in using their Google accounts to save their builds, with all of its information on price and performance, and easily compare it side-by-side to their other custom PC builds.

The Build Guides feature includes viewable information that shows users already-created PC builds by experts that are confirmed to work and be optimized. This serves as a resource for users to compare their recommended builds with.

The Part Selector and Custom PC Builder feature will be the main feature of the website. Using a CSV dataset on recent modern PC part data, we will be able to provide information on each unique component's specifications, but it will be up to us to code and list which parts are compatable with each other. Ideally, we would only allow options which are known to be compatable to be selected by users when creating a custom PC build.

Finally, we have a possible forum feature which may be developed depending on time constraints. This feature would allow for users to upload their own custom builds to show off to others and inspire further optimizations and creativity. If users are struggling to know where to start, the forums might be a great spot to see what other users are doing! As of now, this might be more of a stretch goal to be tackled after the rest of the features are implemented.

Summary

Our website aims to address the struggles PC building newcomers experience when trying to create their own computer. By allowing for users to quickly and effectively make builds to suit their budgets and other constraints, they can be confident in knowing that their custom build will not only work, but also be significantly cheaper than simply buying a pre-built. Further, for users who want to stretch their budget as far as possible, our comparison feature will allow for users to view which components match their desired performance best before buying parts. Finally, our possible feature would be a great spot for users to show off their custom builds, inspire others, and show fellow users what works and does not work with certain customizations.
